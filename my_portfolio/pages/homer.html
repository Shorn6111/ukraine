Welcome to Homer

"The Halibut Capital of the World"

Homer

Originally inhabited by the Inuit tribe and then the Taninia tribe. Established as a coal mining town in 1885 and was anemd after Homer Peacock a Gold prospector. The economy of the town changed from coal to fishing at the turn of the 20th century. Homer's main economic resources are fishing and tourism. Homer serves as a gateway to Kachemak Bay State Park and State Wilderness Park, a popular recreational area (boating, kayaking, fishing, and hiking) containing some 400,000 acres (160,000 hectares) of glaciers, forests, and mountains. Nearby is Kenai National Wildlife Refuge.

Places to Eat

While here in Homer you can have some of the freshest seafood around taken right from the docks to your plate.Some resturants include Boardwalk Fish and Chips, The Tickled Pear, and Captain Pattie's Fish House.

Places to Stay

Here in Homer we have some nice cozy places located on both Homer's spit and downtown Homer. On Homer's spit we have Land's End Resort and Otter Beach Lodges.Off the Spit we have Alaskan Suites and Best Western Birkarda Inn.

Entertainment

Here we have a long skinny Pennisula called Homer's Spit that has been covered in Small Stores and Resturants. While in Homer you can take some deepsea fishing trips out of of docks. You can also go hunting in the surrounding mountains of Homer.
